## SECTION 4: GUIDING PRINCIPLES FOR ALL OUTREACH

1. **Serve first. Always.** The moment we serve in order to recruit, we've lost the plot.

2. **Relationships over attendance.** We measure success by depth of relationship, not headcount.

3. **Be where people are.** Don't ask them to come to us. Go to them. The coffee shop. The festival. The trailhead. The game store.

4. **Respect existing communities.** The library D&D group, the Roughnecks MC, the Farmers Market vendors â€” they were here first. We join, not replace.

5. **Earn trust slowly.** Trust is built in drops and lost in buckets. Show up consistently for 6 months before expecting anyone to care who we are.

6. **No bait-and-switch.** If we say it's a game night, it's a game night. If we say it's a cookout, it's a cookout. We never ambush people with a gospel presentation they didn't sign up for.

7. **Let the Spirit work.** If we are who we say we are â€” authentic, loving, present â€” people will ask questions. We trust God to open doors. We don't kick them down.

8. **Embrace the weird.** The Vine is for people who don't fit in. That means *we* might not look like a "normal" church. Good. Normal church has failed these people. Let's be something different.

9. **Everyone has a gift.** Outreach isn't the pastor's job. It's *everyone's* job, using *their* gifts. The coder hosts Terminal. The biker leads Iron Horse. The gamer runs Respawn. The cook feeds the neighborhood.

10. **Stay rooted.** All of this activity means nothing if we're not connected to the Vine. Prayer, Scripture, worship, discipleship â€” these are the roots. The outreach is the fruit. Don't confuse the two.
