# 🌿 THE VINE — 20-Point Outreach Plan Synopsis

---

## Guiding Philosophy

This is not a church growth strategy — it's a **people strategy**. Built on three convictions:

1. **Go to them.** Don't wait for people to come to us.
2. **Serve first, talk later.** Earn the right to be heard.
3. **Build bridges, not booths.** Relationships over flyers.

Every initiative targets people the traditional church has overlooked: the IT crowd, gamers, bikers, creatives, skeptics, and anyone who's been told they don't belong.

---

## The 20 Points

| # | Name | What It Is | Why It Matters |
|---|------|-----------|----------------|
| 1 | **TERMINAL** | Weekly Code & Coffee at Blue Front Coffee Co. (Wed 8–10 AM) | No tech meetup exists in Athens — fills a real gap for IT workers, students, and remote workers |
| 2 | **RESPAWN** | Monthly public game nights — consoles, tabletop RPGs, board games, cards (1st Friday 6–10 PM) | Local gaming demand is growing but supply is shrinking (Pair A Dice closed); coordinates with library Tabletop Guild |
| 3 | **IRON HORSE** | Monthly motorcycle fellowship rides through Ocoee/Hiwassee corridors (2nd Saturday 9 AM) | SE Tennessee is motorcycle paradise but many riders aren't "club guys" — this is for everyone who rides |
| 4 | **BRANCHES** | Discipleship micro-groups of 3–5 people meeting weekly for honest conversation, Scripture, and prayer | The core engine of The Vine — relationships where you can say "I'm struggling" and be met with grace |
| 5 | **Love Thy Neighbor** | Monthly volunteer partnership with TWU's food insecurity initiative (food bank, mobile pantry) | 1,400+ households served annually in McMinn County food deserts — real need, real service |
| 6 | **BASECAMP** | Monthly outdoor adventures — kayaking, hiking, biking, fishing, camping in the Ocoee region | Church in God's creation; always includes a "chill option" for all fitness levels |
| 7 | **Festival Servant Team** | Volunteer with festival organizers at 6–8 Athens events per year (MooFest, Pumpkintown, etc.) | Show up to serve the festival — trash, setup, water, vendor help — not to set up a church booth |
| 8 | **Tech Help Clinics** | Free monthly tech support at the library (last Saturday 10 AM–1 PM) | Older adults and low-income families struggle with tech but can't afford help — this meets a real need |
| 9 | **AMPLIFIED** | Monthly open worship & jam night — all genres, all skill levels (last Thursday 7–9 PM) | Bridges secular and sacred music; musicians who'd never attend Sunday morning might show up for a jam |
| 10 | **Farmers Market** | Weekly presence during market season — free cold brew, kids activities, or a listening table | Not a church booth — a useful, relational presence where vendors know us by name |
| 11 | **United Way Day of Caring** | Annual one-day volunteer deployment with United Way of McMinn & Meigs Counties | Serves alongside the broader community; builds relationships with partner agencies for year-round support |
| 12 | **Benevolence Network** | Coordinated response to tangible needs — car repairs, plumbing, meals, resume help, childcare | Not charity — neighboring. No hoops, no applications, no "are you a member" check |
| 13 | **Theology on Tap** | Quarterly conversations at a local restaurant/bar on big questions over food and drinks | Topics like "Church hurt is real — now what?" reach people who'd never walk into a Bible study |
| 14 | **TWU Campus Engagement** | Consistent relational presence at Tennessee Wesleyan — events, dinners, resume workshops | Non-predatory; complement TWU's existing campus ministries, don't compete with them |
| 15 | **ROOT ACCESS** | Biweekly men's group built around shared work — woodworking, car repair, welding, electronics | Discipleship happens while working with your hands, not sitting in rows; "root access" = deepest parts of who you are |
| 16 | **WILDFLOWER** | Biweekly women's community — alternating home gatherings and outings (hiking, art, cooking) | Rejects the pastel "women's ministry" stereotype; tackles hard topics; intentionally multigenerational |
| 17 | **Neighborhood Cookouts** | Quarterly cookouts rotating through Athens neighborhoods and apartment complexes | Old-school neighboring — fire up the grill, invite every house on the block, no program |
| 18 | **Nonprofit Volunteerism** | Embedded volunteers at H.O.P.E. Center, Good Faith Clinic, YMCA, CASA, Foster Village, Senior Center | Don't reinvent the wheel — become the most reliable volunteers these organizations have ever had |
| 19 | **Digital Presence** | Discord (primary hub), Instagram, YouTube, website, Facebook — content that doesn't feel like a church account | Meme game must be strong; Discord is where communities live; respond to every DM personally |
| 20 | **The Open Table** | Monthly interest meetings — free lunch, The Vine's story, "ask us anything" Q&A (Sunday 12:30 PM) | Not a membership class — an open table where anyone can explore whether this community is for them |
