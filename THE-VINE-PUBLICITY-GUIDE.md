### Facebook Group Rules of Engagement

1. **Join all of the above groups.** Have 2‚Äì3 Vine members in each one so we're not relying on one person.
2. **Be a member first, a promoter second.** Answer questions. Comment on posts. Help people. Be *known* in the group before you ever post a Vine event.
3. **Respect group rules.** Most have rules about promotional posts. Read them. Follow them. If a group says "no church ads," don't post church ads. Post about a free gaming night or a free tech help clinic ‚Äî that's a community event, not a church ad.
4. **Post as a person, not as a brand.** "Hey everyone, I'm hosting a free game night this Friday ‚Äî board games, console tournaments, snacks. Come hang out!" reads completely different from "The Vine Church invites you to our monthly Respawn event."
5. **Use images.** Facebook's algorithm crushes text-only posts. Every event post needs an eye-catching graphic.
6. **Timing matters.** Post Tuesday‚ÄìThursday for best visibility. Avoid weekends (lower engagement). Post between 9‚Äì11 AM or 6‚Äì8 PM.
7. **Reply to every comment.** Someone asks "What kind of games?" ‚Äî answer within 30 minutes.

## 7. NATIONAL EVENT PLATFORMS

### Meetup
- **URL:** [meetup.com](https://www.meetup.com/)
- **Athens page:** [meetup.com/find/us--tn--athens](https://www.meetup.com/find/us--tn--athens)
- **Cost:** Organizer subscription ~$17/month (free for attendees)
- **Use for:** Create a "The Vine Athens" group or separate groups: "Athens Code & Coffee" (Terminal), "Athens Game Night" (Respawn), "SE TN Motorcycle Rides" (Iron Horse). Meetup indexes well in Google and reaches people *actively searching* for community.
- **Key insight:** Athens has almost NO active Meetup groups. Creating one for any of these niches puts The Vine at the top of search results for anyone in the area looking for community.

### Google Business Profile
- **URL:** [business.google.com](https://business.google.com/)
- **Cost:** Free
- **Use for:** Register The Vine as a local church/organization. This controls the Google Maps listing, Knowledge Panel, and local search results. **Critical** for "churches near me" searches.
- **Actions:** Add photos, service times, events (Google Posts), website link, phone number. Encourage reviews from members.

### Google Events
- Events posted to your Google Business Profile appear in Google Search results when people search for events in Athens. Free and automatic if you use Google Posts.

### Flyer Design Principles

- **Keep it simple.** One event per flyer. Large font. Minimal text.
- **Include a QR code** linking to Eventbrite/Discord/website ‚Äî bridges physical to digital
- **On-brand design:** Dark background, vine green accent, JetBrains Mono font. Match the website aesthetic.
- **No churchy clip art.** No crosses. No stock photos of diverse smiling people. Clean, modern, tech-adjacent design.
- **Tear-off tabs** at the bottom with the event URL or Discord invite link
- **Size:** Standard 8.5"x11" for bulletin boards. 4"x6" postcards for counter cards.

## 15. WORD OF MOUTH & RELATIONAL INVITATIONS

The most powerful channel of all. Research consistently shows that **personal invitation** is the #1 reason people visit a new church or community group. All the billboards and Facebook posts in the world don't match a friend saying "Hey, you should come with me this Friday."

### Equipping Vine Members for Natural Invitations

- **Give them language.** Many people want to invite friends but don't know what to say. Provide simple scripts:
  - "We do this game night thing once a month ‚Äî free snacks, console tournaments, board games. You should come."
  - "A bunch of us ride through the Ocoee on Saturdays. You ride? Come join."
  - "Some of us meet for coffee and coding on Wednesdays. It's super chill. You'd fit right in."
- **Give them tools.** Business-card-sized invite cards with:
  - Event name and date
  - Location
  - QR code to Discord or Eventbrite
  - Website URL
  - Tagline: "where misfits find family"
- **Normalize invitation.** Talk about it from the platform: "Who are you going to bring next month?" Not as guilt ‚Äî as excitement.
- **Personal follow-up.** When someone visits, a Vine member texts them within 24 hours: "It was great meeting you. Hope you had fun. You're always welcome."

### The "Plus One" Challenge
Once per quarter, challenge every Vine member to bring one friend to one event ‚Äî not Sunday, just any event. Respawn, Basecamp, a cookout. Lower the barrier. Track results.

## 19. DESIGN STANDARDS & BRAND VOICE

Every piece of publicity must feel like it came from the same place. Consistency builds recognition.

### Visual Identity

| Element | Standard |
|---|---|
| **Primary colors** | Background: `#0a0a0f` (near black). Accent: `#5cb85c` (vine green). Secondary: `#d4a843` (warm amber). |
| **Typography** | Headers: Space Grotesk (Bold). Body: Space Grotesk (Regular). Accent/Labels: JetBrains Mono. Handwritten: Caveat. |
| **Logo** | SVG vine-in-leaf icon (from website). Used small, never dominant. |
| **Photography** | Real people. Real moments. Dark/moody lighting. Never stock photos. |
| **Tone** | Warm, direct, slightly irreverent, never cheesy. Like talking to a smart friend. |

### Voice Guidelines

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "Come hang out with us" | "We invite you to join us for worship" |
| "No dress code. No judgment." | "All are welcome at our family-friendly services" |
| "Bring your questions. All of them." | "Discover the answers you've been searching for" |
| "Free snacks. Trash talk encouraged." | "Refreshments will be provided" |
| "We're coders and bikers, gamers and grandparents" | "We are a diverse and inclusive congregation" |
| Use lowercase. Use contractions. Use humor. | Use formal language. Use church jargon. Use clip art. |

### What NOT to Put on Publicity

- ‚ùå Stock photos of smiling diverse families
- ‚ùå Crosses, doves, or stained glass imagery
- ‚ùå Bible verses as the main headline (fine in small print or inside)
- ‚ùå The word "fellowship" (too churchy ‚Äî use "community" or "crew")
- ‚ùå "Join us this Sunday!" as the primary CTA (too generic)
- ‚ùå Pastel colors, script fonts, or anything that looks like a women's retreat brochure
