## Phase 1 — Foundation

### Months 1–3 | Goal: $0 to $30,000

*The entire purpose of Phase 1 is speed to the district match trigger. Every dollar here counts double.*

---

### 1. Personal Support Team

**Target: $12,000–$15,000**

Build a list of 50–75 people who know you personally — friends, family, ministry colleagues, former congregants, fellow pastors. These are people who trust *you*, not a brochure.

| Action | Details |
|:-------|:--------|
| **Personal letters** | Handwritten or personally addressed (not mass mail). Tell your story, the vision for Athens, and a specific ask: *"I'm asking 50 people to give $250 each."* |
| **Follow-up calls** | Within 10 days of each letter. Not to pressure — to connect. Most people who intend to give never do because nobody followed up. |
| **Monthly pledge option** | $50/month for 12 months = $600/year. Lower barrier, steady income stream. |

> **Expected response**: 30–40% at an average of $300–$500 per household.

---

### 2. Church Partnership Appeals

**Target: $10,000–$15,000**

Other churches in your network — district, denomination, and friendly independent congregations. You're not competing with them; you're extending the mission.

| Strategy | Ask | Expected Yield |
|:---------|:----|---------------:|
| **Pulpit visits** (8–12 churches) | 5-minute vision cast during service | $1,000–$2,500 per church |
| **Adopt-a-Month** | Church covers one month of operating costs | $500–$1,000 per church |
| **Prayer + giving partnerships** | Weekly prayer commitment | Builds relationship; giving often follows |

---

### 3. Pastor & Leader Network

**Target: $3,000–$5,000**

Fellow pastors, district leaders, and ministry friends who understand the cost of church planting. A personal ask — coffee, phone call, or Zoom. Many pastors will give $100–$500 personally when asked by a peer they respect.

## Phase 2 — Expansion

### Months 3–6 | Goal: $30,000 to $60,000

*District match is triggered. You have $50,000 in hand. Momentum is real and visible — use it.*

---

### 4. Vision Dinners

**Target: $10,000–$15,000**

Small gatherings of 10–20 people in someone's home. Not a fundraiser — a dinner where you share the vision.

| Element | Details |
|:--------|:--------|
| **Format** | 90 minutes total |
| **Meal** | Hosted by a supporter who covers food costs |
| **Presentation** | 20-minute vision cast + 10 minutes Q&A |
| **The ask** | Pledge cards on the table — private, no one put on the spot |
| **Social proof** | A supporter who's already given shares *why* they gave |
| **Quantity** | Host 4–6 dinners across different networks |

> **Key insight**: The cards are private. No public asking. No pressure. People respond to vision, not guilt.

---

### 5. Grants — Denominational & Parachurch

**Target: $5,000–$10,000**

Many funds go unused because nobody applies. Don't leave this money on the table.

| Source | Type | Notes |
|:-------|:-----|:------|
| **Denominational planting fund** | Grant | Beyond the loan — ask your district superintendent about grant programs |
| **Church planting networks** | Grant | Stadia, ARC, Exponential, or denomination-specific equivalents |
| **Christian foundations** | Grant | Lilly Endowment, Louisville Institute, smaller regional foundations |
| **Local community foundations** | Grant | McMinn County or Bradley County foundations fund community-building initiatives |

---

### 6. Athens Community Investment

**Target: $5,000–$8,000**

You're not just planting a church — you're investing in a community. Frame it that way.

- **Local business sponsorships**: Host free community events (block party, family movie night, back-to-school supply drive). Local businesses sponsor for visibility; you get goodwill and a platform to share the vision.
- **Service-first approach**: Before you ask Athens for anything, serve Athens. Organize volunteers for park cleanups, food bank shifts, or nonprofit partnerships. Generosity generates generosity.

## Phase 3 — Completion

### Months 6–12 | Goal: $60,000 to $80,000

---

### 7. Recurring Giving Base

**Target: $10,000–$15,000**

By now you have a core team forming. These are people who will *be* the church.

| Platform Options | Notes |
|:-----------------|:------|
| Tithe.ly | Purpose-built for churches, low fees |
| Pushpay | Strong mobile experience |
| PayPal / Venmo | Familiar, zero setup friction |

> **Core team math**: 15–20 families at $200/month = $3,000–$4,000/month.
> Over 6 months = **$18,000–$24,000**. Even at half that, it's significant.

This isn't "fundraising" — this is the beginning of the church's financial life.

---

### 8. Second-Circle Expansion

**Target: $5,000–$8,000**

Your supporters know people you don't. Equip them to share.

| Channel | Approach |
|:--------|:---------|
| **Peer-to-peer** | Give supporters a one-page overview to share with *their* networks |
| **Social media** | Regular story updates — not asks. Photos of the team serving. Short video walking through Athens. |
| **Crowdfunding** | 30-day campaign on GiveSendGo with a specific goal: *"$5,000 to secure our first meeting space"* |

---

### 9. Matching Gift Challenges

**Target: $3,000–$5,000**

Find one or two generous supporters willing to match.

```
  "A supporter will match every dollar given this month, up to $2,500."

  Donor gives $100  ------>  Becomes $200
  Donor gives $500  ------>  Becomes $1,000
  Total impact:              DOUBLED
```

Even small matches ($500–$1,000) drive action. People love the feeling of multiplied impact.

## Guiding Principles

> **1. Ask with clarity and confidence.**
> You're not begging — you're inviting people into something God is doing.
> *"$250 helps us run for one week"* beats *"please give generously"* every time.

> **2. Follow up relentlessly but graciously.**
> Most giving happens after the second or third contact. A "just checking in" call
> two weeks after a dinner converts more pledges than the dinner itself.

> **3. Steward publicly.**
> Quarterly updates to every donor — what their money did, where the church is,
> what's next. People who feel informed give again.

> **4. Separate the ask from the relationship.**
> If someone says no, thank them sincerely and keep the friendship.
> Some of your best future supporters will be people who said no the first time.

> **5. Keep the $50K loan in your back pocket.**
> If you hit $80K and launch strong, you won't need it. If an unexpected opportunity
> arises — a perfect building — it's there. Options without obligations.

## What This Plan Avoids

| Avoided | Why |
|:--------|:----|
| Selling merchandise or products | Transactional, not relational |
| Quid-pro-quo ("give and get") | Cheapens the mission |
| Guilt-based appeals | Manipulative, short-term thinking |
| MLM-adjacent structures | Erodes trust |
| Ticketed "benefit" events | Masks donations as purchases |

**Every dollar comes from people who believe in the mission and choose to invest in it freely. That's how a church should start.**
